# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

Project participation must remain respectful, constructive, and safe. Good
participation includes assuming good intent, discussing technical tradeoffs
with evidence, accepting correction, respecting boundaries, and making space
for people with different backgrounds and experience levels.

Harassment, threats, discrimination, sexualized attention, deliberate
intimidation, doxxing, sustained disruption, and publication of another
person's private information are unacceptable.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions,
release channels, and private project communication. It also applies when a
participant publicly represents the project.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to the repository owner through the contact
method on their GitHub profile. Do not open a public issue containing private
or identifying information. Reports will be reviewed in context and shared
only as needed to investigate and act.

Maintainers may edit or remove contributions, issue a warning, temporarily
restrict participation, or permanently ban a participant. Retaliation against
someone who reports a concern is itself a violation.
